Sustainability in Healthcare

Sustainability in healthcare involves creating systems that provide high-quality care while minimizing environmental impact and ensuring long-term viability. This includes reducing resource consumption, managing waste responsibly, and adopting energy-efficient technologies. Sustainable healthcare also promotes preventive care and wellness, aiming to reduce the overall burden on healthcare systems by focusing on early intervention and education. Additionally, it emphasizes equity, ensuring that all individuals have access to necessary care without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their healthcare needs.

COVID-19 Long-Term Impact Studies

Lifecare Hospital is actively researching the long-term effects of COVID-19, with a focus on symptoms like fatigue, respiratory issues, cardiovascular complications, and cognitive problems. Known as "Long COVID," these persistent symptoms affect many patients even months after recovery. Our team of specialists, including cardiologists, pulmonologists, and neurologists, is working to understand the virus’s long-term impact on the body. Through ongoing research, Lifecare Hospital aims to develop effective treatment plans and provide personalized care to help patients regain their quality of life.

International Patient Care

LifeCare Hospital has a dedicated team to handle the care of international patients within the hospital. This includes coordinating not only the patient’s stay at the hospital and insurance coverage but also airport drop off and pickups as well as hotel accommodation.
